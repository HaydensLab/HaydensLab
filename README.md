### 🦠 Pathogens
I love pathogens.... not in a weird way I just think as aspects of life they are some of the best out their, highly adaptable and endlessly varied. Both my undergrad and postgrad research projects have been about pathogens. First, in undergrad I started working with a microscope I had bought to produce some hopefully pleasing videos, timelapses and images of microscopic pond life: [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/the_microsphere). In my dissertation I was involved in identification of a novel parasite of the copepod (Planktonic crustacean _Calanus finmarchicus_) through _Eukarya_ wide phylogenetics of pathogenic transcripts I filtered out by extensive BLASTing.

### ♋ Cancer genomics
I then went on to do my Master's in Cancer biology and Immunology continuing at the University of Southampton. I was able in my project to research HPV viral genomics incomporating all I had learned previously performing post-hoc viral lineage analysis by phylogenetics, analysing viral genomic variation between patients. I have worked with FFPE filtered data complemented a parallel human analysis (of the human exome alongside these viral genomes) in Oropharyngeal squamous cell carcinoma (OPSCC).
